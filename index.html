Our Solar System

The Sun is a massive, glowing sphere of hot gas at the center of the Solar System, acting as its primary source of light and heat. Composed mostly of hydrogen and helium, the Sunâ€™s immense gravity holds the planets in orbit and fuels the energy needed for life on Earth. Its core is where nuclear fusion occurs, converting hydrogen into helium and releasing a tremendous amount of energy in the process.


A fun fact about the Sun is that it accounts for over 99% of the total mass in the Solar System. In other words, if all the planets, moons, asteroids, and comets were put together, they would still be less than 1% of the Sun's mass.
